Life Skills

Communication Skills

An Exchange of information from one person to another is called Communication. It can be audio, visual, writing or combination thereof. You are likely to interact with number of people in your life and Good Communication Skills can improve your relationships by helping you to understand others, and to be understood.

Good eye contact, body language, active listening are the tools of effective communication.

Time Management

We are all busy with so many things in our life that keep us occupied for doing anything else, but a question that we should ask ourselves, is this schedule taking us closer to our goal every day?

Time Management is a skill to use your time in an effective way that we achieve what we really want. It is easy to make schedules and timetables, but where we lack is EXECUTION.

Emotional Intelligence

Emotional Intelligence (EI) is Emotional Quotient (EQ) of a person. Emotional Intelligence is not about being Emotional; it’s about being smart with your emotions.

Emotional Intelligence means to understand our own emotions and those of others and knowing how to express your emotions appropriately.

Emotional Intelligence helps to solve the interpersonal problem and regulate the behavior. It can help you to build an optimal relationship and produce desired outcomes.

Decision Making

Decision Making is a process of judgment where you choose a best option for particular situation considering all the alternatives. For effective decision making a person must be able to see all the outcomes (positives and negatives) and select the option which is best for that particular decision.

Goal, Risk, Priority are some of the factors which one should consider before making any decision.

“Be sure you are right -- then go ahead" - Davy Crocket

Critical Thinking

A process of making reasoned judgement which is logical and well thought. Critical thinking is part of decision making which is based on evidence and information instead of unwarranted assumptions or biases.

Critical thinking is disciplined process to actively examining and analysing the information gathered by reasoning and experience. It requires curiosity, criticism, scepticism and modesty.

Skill of Learning

Skill of Learning or Learning skill is ability to “LEARN TO LEARN”

Learning is a continuous process that commences at birth and continues until death. Learning is not limited to study or thinking, it involves trust, value, will, and emotions. The response or feedback is a powerful tool to know whether it is working or not.
